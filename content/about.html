Who Am I?

I am an easy going guy, who likes to brainstorm up crazy ideas. I typically like to analyze situations to come up with the best solution. Obviously there are scenarios that I do not know what is best to do, which gets my research gears turning in overdrive. In software this trait works much to my advantage because I want to do my best.

My passion for all things computer and software started way back in elementary school. Console video games started to arise and I grasped them quickly. Eventually my parents got a basic computer (I believe it was a Pentium) and I began to dabble with that. Sometime not long after that point I had already decided for myself that I would pursue a career that involves programming (originally aiming for game development).

Education

During my high-school years I started to take my growing interest in software programming more seriously. I took all the computer programming courses available and I did some readings at home. Eventually the time for University came, I decided to go to University of Ontario Institute of Technology (UOIT) and completed my B.Eng in Software Engineering in 2010.

During the last two years of my undergraduate degree I was engaged in research work with Dr. Jeremy S. Bradbury. My research focus was on software quality and testing of concurrent (multi-threaded) programs. I thoroughly enjoyed doing research, especially in this area. I decided to stick with it and pursue a Masters in Computer Science at UOIT with Dr. Jeremy S. Bradbury.

I completed my Master degree on Predicting Mutation Scores using Source Code and Test Suite Metrics at UOIT. I was originally thinking of pursuing a PhD degree after my Masters, but I decided to gain some industry experience first. I might come back to research eventually, but only time will tell.

I am currently working at Fixmo as a Software Engineer. At Fixmo I primarily work in front-end development using Vaadin. In my spare cycles I have introduced and established automated UI testing of our product.
